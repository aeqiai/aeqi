## Design Context

### Users
Technical founders and developers who want to multiply their output with autonomous AI agents. They create "companies" — workspaces where agents write code, review PRs, research markets, and build products autonomously. They're competent, time-constrained, and expect tools that respect their intelligence. When they land on AEQI, they should feel: "this is serious infrastructure, not a toy."

### Brand Personality
**Precise. Autonomous. Inevitable.**

AEQI is infrastructure for the future of work. Not a chatbot, not an assistant — an autonomous runtime. The interface should feel like a command center for something powerful running beneath the surface. Quiet confidence, not flashy promises.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Monochromatic, light mode, typography-driven. Black and white with intentional use of space. Density where it serves function, breathing room where it creates clarity.
- **References**: Linear (precision, monochromatic, purposeful), Vercel (developer authority, stark black/white), Stripe (institutional quality, typographic hierarchy).
- **Anti-references**: NOT crypto dashboards (neon, dark mode, gradient text). NOT Notion (playful, emoji, illustrations). NOT generic SaaS (blue gradients, stock photos, rounded cards). NOT hacker aesthetic (terminal green, Matrix vibes). NOT AI slop (cyan-on-dark, purple gradients, glassmorphism, bouncing animations).
- **Theme**: Light mode only. Pure monochromatic palette — black text on white, opacity-based grays. No color except status indicators (success green, error red).
- **Typography**: Inter for UI, JetBrains Mono for code/data. No decorative fonts.

### Design Principles
1. **Density over decoration** — Show information, not chrome. Every pixel earns its place.
2. **Systematic, not stylistic** — Design decisions come from the system (spacing scale, type scale, token palette), not ad-hoc choices.
3. **Professional restraint** — The product is powerful; the interface doesn't need to prove it. No gratuitous animation, no visual noise, no marketing speak inside the app.
4. **Typography is the design** — Hierarchy, weight, and spacing do the heavy lifting. Icons support, they don't lead.
5. **Respect the user** — No onboarding slides, no tooltips explaining obvious things, no empty states that patronize. Users are smart; the interface should match.
